Profile

Twenty years spent on both sides of the regulatory table — as in-house counsel, as a government evaluator, and as outside advisor to global institutions operating in Korea.

Taejin Um is a Seoul-based senior attorney and policy professional whose career spans in-house counsel, government relations, regulatory advisory, AI and copyright policy, and international commercial contracting. His differentiator is structural: an active U.S. legal credential (Washington D.C. Bar, since 2004) paired with sustained, direct access to Korean regulatory bodies — MCST, KCC, KFTC, MND, and DAPA among them.

He currently serves as COO and Principal Consultant at K-Link Global, External AI Policy Advisor at Wesley Quest, Adjunct Professor and Chair of the ICT & Public Policy Research Lab at Yonsei University's Graduate School of Public Administration, and Advisor / Of Counsel at ClassHankyul Law Group.
